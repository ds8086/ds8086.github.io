minecraft

I do not recall exactly when I started playing Minecraft, I remember horses being big news at some point though... I rarely play any more, but on occasion I wax nostalgic. Someone will spin up a Spigot or Paper server with the latest in quality of life mods and we will spend a few weeks building, mining, and trying to recall potion recipes. My second-hand precision workstation has 64GB of RAM and a decent nvidia Quadro GPU. Minecraft runs great...but almighty CMOS! Deliver me from the Microsoft provided Minecraft launcher. The fans spin up, videos start playing, and I am bombarded with opportunities to spend money on a game I already purchased.† It is sensory overload. I think about it and my interest to play Minecraft evaporates. Let's fix that.

installation

Run the Minecraft launcher as you normally would. From the Installations tab find your desired Minecraft version and click Play. Why version 1.7.10 for me? The last major release before Microsoft bought Mojang.

With Minecraft launched, leave the game sitting on the title screen.

batch

Launch a PowerShell 7 instance and run the command below to create the batch file which will later be used to launch your selected Minecraft installation. 

        
(Get-Process javaw).CommandLine | Out-File ~\Desktop\minecraft.bat

account

If Minecraft is the only reason your Microsoft account exists in Windows, then why not remove it? Open Windows settings via Win + i and navigate to Accounts > Your accounts. Locate your Microsoft account and click Remove.‡

With your Microsoft account removed from Windows, run the Minecraft launcher again. This is to demonstrate that while you are not signed in via a Microsoft account, you can still play Minecraft. You will be prompted for a Microsoft sign-in, just close the prompt via the X.

The Minecraft launcher will continue but you will quickly be met with another sign-in prompt. Close the Minecraft Launcher.

play

Time to play Minecraft! Double-click the previously created batch file on your desktop or run the PowerShell command below. 

        
Start-Process -Path ~\Desktop\minecraft.bat -WindowStyle Hidden

Look at that; Minecraft without a Microsoft sign-in and without the official launcher.

faq

Q: Is this illegal?
A: I'm an engineer, not an attorney. Many third-party Minecraft launchers exist, you've just made your own.

Q: What if I want a different version?
A: Repeat the process, update the name of the batch file created.

Q: What if I want the latest version?
A: Use a Minecraft launcher, either official or third-party. This solution isn't really for you.

Q: Can I connect to Minecraft servers?
A: Probably. I haven't tried yet.

Q: What about Bedrock edition?
A: I don't know how to help you.
